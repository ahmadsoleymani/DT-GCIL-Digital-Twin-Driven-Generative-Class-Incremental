This letter proposes DT-GCIL, a federated defense framework driven by Digital Twin (DT) that provides a continuous adaptation to the 'Internet of Vehicles (IoV). In DT-GCIL, low-confidence or out-of-distribution samples detected by autonomous vehicles are forwarded to the DT for validation. Validated novel attacks trigger classifier-head expansion, while GAN-based replay preserves previous attack knowledge and mitigates catastrophic forgetting. Trust-aware federated aggregation integrates Autonomous Vehicles (AVs) and DT updates, and only lightweight classifier-head and prototype increments are disseminated to reduce communication cost. Experiments using sequential attack scenarios show that DT-GCIL achieves detection performance close to fully trained FL, significantly reduces forgetting compared with Regularization-CIL, and provides a stronger accuracy--communication trade-off for real-time IoV deployment.
